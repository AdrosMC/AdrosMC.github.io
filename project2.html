Pong by Adrian Geiss

Why Pong?

The game Pong is an absolute classic. As one of the first video games, it has been thrilling vast numbers of gamers for decades. Yet there is not much to Pong. All it does is move a ball from one side of the screen to the other, thrown back by two moving bars. But that's where the charm of Pong comes from. It is simple in programming as well as in understanding the game, but at the same time it offers a perfect introduction to the world of video games or, more generally, to developing.
Therefore, it was our task in the course "Coding Essentials 1" to re-code this legend. We also had to incorporate our own ideas and thus create our own variation. My variation was a "block-breaker" mode, where a certain number of blocks appear that can be destroyed by touching the ball. The aim is to hit all the blocks before the ball goes out three times.
